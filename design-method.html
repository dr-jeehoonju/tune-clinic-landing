The Process

Anatomical Design Workflow

Every procedure begins with a precise, physician-led mapping process directly on your skin.

Step 01

Baseline Assessment

Evaluating underlying skin condition, tissue thickness, and resting facial tension before any markings begin.

Step 02

Interactive Planning

Collaborating with you using a mirror to align your aesthetic goals with realistic anatomical possibilities.

Step 03

Primary Vector Mapping

Tracing the foundational lifting vectors and identifying the primary direction of tissue laxity.

Step 04

Structural Blueprint

Completing the first-stage design to map out the exact zones requiring deep energy delivery or support.

Step 05

Micro-Detailing

Isolating secondary areas of concern, such as localized volume loss or delicate skin zones that require specific focus.

Safety Protocol
Step 06

Depth & Safety Calibration

Applying final clinical markers (red) to delineate precise treatment depths and strictly avoid anatomical danger zones.
